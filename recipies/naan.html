Naan Recipe

Description:

Naan bread is a type of leavened, oven-baked flatbread that originated in South Asia, particularly within Indian cuisine.
Known for its soft, chewy texture and slightly crispy exterior, naan is typically made from a simple dough consisting of flour, yeast, water, yogurt, and a bit of oil or ghee (clarified butter).
The dough is traditionally left to rise, rolled out, and then cooked in a tandoor, which is a clay oven, giving the bread its distinctive charred spots and smoky flavor.


Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp active dry yeast
  • 2 tbsp plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
  • 1/2 cup warm water (110°F / 45°C)

Steps:

  1. Activate Yeast:Dissolve 1 tsp sugar in 1/2 cup warm water, sprinkle 1/2 tsp yeast, and let it sit until frothy (10 mins).
  2. Make Dough: Mix 2 cups flour, 1 tsp salt, 2 tbsp yogurt, 2 tbsp oil or ghee, and the yeast mixture. Knead until smooth.
  3. Rise: Place dough in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise until doubled (1-2 hours).
  4. Shape: Divide dough into 6-8 balls, roll into ovals about 1/4 inch thick.
  5. Cook: Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook each naan for 1-2 minutes per side until golden and bubbly.
  6. Finish: Brush hot naan with melted butter or ghee.
